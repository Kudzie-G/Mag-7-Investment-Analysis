#  RESULT SUMMARY
##  Key Insights

### 1. **Portfolio Growth**
- The portfolio showed **steady upward growth** from ~£8,000 in 2020 to ~£12,000 by 2025.  
- Growth was driven primarily by **NVDA, MSFT, and TSLA**, which outperformed peers.

---

### 2. **Drawdowns & Risk**
- Significant drawdowns occurred during **2022 market volatility**, reflecting tech-sector sensitivity.  
- Recovery was strong in 2023–2024, highlighting resilience of mega-cap tech stocks.  
- **TSLA and NVDA** contributed most to volatility swings, while **MSFT and AAPL** provided stability.

---

### 3. **Rolling Volatility**
- Volatility peaked during **macro shocks (2022 inflation, rate hikes)**.  
- **NVDA and TSLA** showed the highest rolling volatility, consistent with their growth-stock profiles.  
- **AAPL and MSFT** remained relatively stable, acting as defensive anchors.

---

### 4. **Top Contributors by Month**
- **NVDA** dominated monthly contributions during AI-driven rallies in 2023–2024.  
- **TSLA** showed sharp but inconsistent contributions, alternating between strong gains and steep losses.  
- **MSFT and AAPL** provided consistent, moderate contributions across months.

---

### 5. **Monthly Returns Heatmap**
- Clear seasonality observed:
  - **Strong Q1 returns** in multiple years (earnings-driven rallies).  
  - **Weak mid-year months** (June–July) with frequent negative returns.  
- **NVDA and TSLA** had the widest swings, while **GOOGL and META** showed mixed performance.

---

### 6. **Relative Performance (Indexed)**
- **NVDA** far outpaced peers, reflecting exponential growth in AI and GPU demand.  
- **TSLA** showed high volatility but long-term upward trajectory.  
- **MSFT and AAPL** delivered steady compounding returns, reinforcing their role as portfolio stabilizers.  
- **AMZN, META, and GOOGL** lagged slightly, reflecting slower growth relative to NVDA and TSLA.

---

## Overall Conclusion
- The **Mag 7 portfolio delivered strong cumulative returns**, with NVDA and TSLA as high-growth drivers but also major sources of volatility.  
- **MSFT and AAPL** acted as stabilizers, balancing risk and smoothing portfolio performance.
